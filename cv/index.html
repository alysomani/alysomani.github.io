WORKING PAPERS

The Kids Are All Right: Parental Job Loss and Children’s Academic Achievement (JMP)
This paper investigates the effect of parental layoff on children’s academic achievement in grades 4, 7, and 10, using administrative data from British Columbia. I find no significant effects on children's test scores due to parental job loss. I also find that matching only on parent/household level variables can still lead to selection into layoff on unobservables that are correlated with children's academic ability.

Majority Reaction to Minority Representation in Local Government
This paper studies the racial dynamics in city council elections in California. I find that in cities where a non-white candidate won a close election against a white candidate, compared to cities where the non-white candidate lost, more white candidates enter the race in the next election.
